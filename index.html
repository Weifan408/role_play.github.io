Role-Play: Learning Adaptive Role-Specific Strategies in Multi-Agent Interactions

Weifan Long, Wen Wen, Zhai Peng, Lihua Zhang
Code arXiv

Abstract

Zero-shot multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) problems which require agents to adapt to unseen agents have attracted increasing attention. Traditional approaches often build a policy pool through Self-Play (SP) framework, and then train the final agent using this pool. However, these frameworks can fall short in capturing the full spectrum of potential strategies, particularly in real-world scenarios with mixed incentives, similar to those encountered in mixed-motive games. The complexity of mixed-motive games requires a more diverse training partner population because the imperfect correlation of incentives among group members demands strategies that can adapt to varying and often conflicting goals.Drawing inspiration from Social Value Orientation (SVO), in which individuals maintain stable orientations in interactions with others, we propose a novel framework named Role-Play (RP). RP employing role embedding to converts the challenge of population diversity into a more manageable diversity of roles. It trains a common policy with a role embedding observation and uses a role predictor to estimate the joint role embeddings of other agents, aiding the learning agent in adapting to its assigned role. We theoretically prove the necessity of the role predictor in this process. We demonstrate the performance of RP on cooperative games (Overcooked) and mixed-motive games (Harvest, CleanUp). Our results show that RP consistently outperforms strong baselines in scenarios involving unseen agents, demonstrating its robustness and adaptability in diverse interaction settings.

Algorithm Framework

Role Play framework, agents use a role predictor to estimate the joint role embeddings of other agents and make decisions independently based on their own role embeddings.

RL^2 based RP, agents sampling their role embeddings at the beginning of each trail and learn to adapt to the roles.
